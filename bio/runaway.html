  • Running Away

When I was 14 (3 years after my mother was killed) I decided to run away. It wasn't anything to do with my adopted family but I was really tired of the place and every time I went past where my mom was killed it brought up some very difficult things. Even almost 30 years later I still tear up and start bawling when I drive past that god damned hill where her car came to a stop after being shot in the head while driving down the highway.

I headed north and someone offered me a place to stay. It was my first encounter with a group called "Ananda Marga" and something that would change my life forever. I eventually became involved in one of their groups called "AMURT". With AMURT and Ananda Marga I would learn a lot about the world and dig in and help with various relief efforts they worked on. One was a famous train collision in NC. Others were earthquakes and floods in Haiti and Louisiana as well as a few other projects as we heard about them. Ananda Marga is a spiritual practice rooted in Shaivite Hinduism and is more about Humanism (or neo-humanism as many call it) than dogma. I encourage you to look into it.
