WARNING: Some of this may be triggering to some people who read it, I apologize in advance. Feel free to skip over this one


After staying with Ananda Marga for a bit and learning about meditation, yoga, and natural remedies and all that stuff. I picked up a book on a bookshelf one day and began reading it. The book was called "The Science Of Self-Realization" By A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupad, leader of ISKCON (International Society of Krsna Consciousness) the book was nice, it had some good arguments and Vedic references. It turned me onto another book the "Bhagavad Gita". I was planning on heading West for a band I had heard of called the Grateful Dead and I heard there was a ISKCON place there in Los Angeles so I made plans to stop in.


Turns out that would be a great decision in ways and a terrible one in others. I found this little temple and went inside. After talking to some of the people in there and discussing things late into the night they offered me some food and a place to sleep for the night so I accepted (Los Angeles is a pretty scary place when you are all alone on the street at 14). I got up the next morning for their morning routine and enjoyed it so much I signed up to stay there for a while and learn more about "Bhakti Yoga" as they described it.


I went on to live in various temples and ashrams through the years. Some were wonderful experiences and some were sheer terror. Like the one in New Vrindavan, I won't go into too many details but suffice it to say devoteeA wanted to have sex with devoteeB's wife and since he wouldn't give permission devoteeA had him killed. But the killer (devoteeC who was paid by devoteeD) botched the job and wound up killing the man and his wife and putting them in a freezer. They also got busted for having a stash of weapons and bomb making materials right by the lake (ghat) and got in some serious troubles for many years. There was sexual assualt, child molestation and rape, abuse of many types going on there. What an insane experience. You can read more about what was going on in New Vrindavan on this website
